# Communication Norms

## Status Updates

- **Audience:** VP Product (Dana Park), EM leads (Chris Okafor for Adventurer Experience, Aisha Nkomo for Guide Experience), Head of Marketing (Priya Anand), cross-functional partners
- **Cadence:** Biweekly — every other Tuesday, same day as sprint close
- **Format:** Written async post in Slack (#product-updates), not slides. 3–5 paragraphs. Leads with delivery status and risk, not activity.
- **Required sections:** Sprint goal + outcome (hit / partial / miss + why), current risks or blockers, what's coming next sprint, any decisions needed from leadership
- **Optional:** Metric callouts when a notable shift occurred; dependency flags for cross-squad work
- **Dana's preference:** Read async; does not want to be walked through it in a meeting. Will Slack DM with follow-up questions if something is unclear or concerning.

## Async Communication

- **Primary channel:** Slack. Terrain uses Slack for all day-to-day communication.
- **Key channels:**
  - `#product-updates` — biweekly status posts; major launch announcements
  - `#terrain-product` — async standups; sprint planning links; day-to-day PM/engineering coordination
  - `#terrain-dev` — engineering channel; PM should monitor but not spam; use for infra questions
  - `#product-design` — design syncs; Figma links; review requests
  - `#incidents` — production alerts; P0/P1 issues; PM should acknowledge and coordinate here during incidents
- **Email vs. Slack:** Slack for anything time-sensitive or internal. Email for external partners (insurance vendors, content partners) and formal legal/compliance communications.
- **Expected response times:** Slack DM — same business day. Channel mention — 2–4 hours during work hours. Email — 24 hours.
- **Async vs. meeting:** Default to async for updates, decisions, and questions that don't require real-time discussion. Schedule a meeting when: live debate is needed, a decision requires reading body language, or more than 3 people need to align simultaneously.

## Writing Norms

- **Lead with the point.** Status updates and briefs open with the key fact or decision, not context-setting.
- **Name the risk explicitly.** Don't bury a risk in the third paragraph. If something is at risk, say so in the first sentence.
- **No passive voice for ownership.** "Engineering will own X" not "X will be handled." Name the person.
- **Slack messages are permanent record.** Don't put anything in Slack you wouldn't put in a doc. For decisions, follow up with a Notion note.
