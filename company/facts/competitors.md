# Competitive Landscape

## Key Competitors

**Airbnb Experiences**
- Positioning: "Activities hosted by locals" — attached to Airbnb's accommodation traffic
- ICP overlap: Adventurers booking travel-adjacent experiences; guides who also host or want destination travelers
- Take rate: 20% from hosts
- Primary threat vector: Demand-side distribution — Airbnb has 150M+ users who see Experiences while booking accommodation. Terrain has no equivalent demand aggregator.
- Current status: Growing but inconsistent quality reputation; Airbnb has deprioritized Experiences as a strategic bet post-COVID
- Terrain differentiator: Outdoor-specific curation and verification; higher quality bar for guides; adventurers can book without being an Airbnb user; better-suited for standalone experience intent (not attached to travel)

**Viator (TripAdvisor)**
- Positioning: "The world's largest experiences marketplace" — dominated by tour operators and professional companies
- ICP overlap: Adventurers in destination mode (traveling to a city/region and looking for activities); operators running structured tours
- Take rate: 20–30% from operators
- Primary threat vector: Scale and SEO dominance — Viator shows up in search for nearly every destination + activity query
- Current status: Large, growing, but built for operators not independent guides
- Terrain differentiator: Independent guides can onboard in under 1 hour; no operator-scale requirement; more authentic and local feel; better for small-group and individual instruction formats

**GetYourGuide**
- Positioning: "Unforgettable experiences around the world"
- ICP overlap: Adventurers in major travel destinations; emerging US market
- Take rate: 20–30%
- Primary threat vector: European market dominance and US expansion; strong brand recognition in travel-aware segments
- Current status: Growing aggressively in North America; primarily cultural (food tours, museum experiences, city walks) with thin outdoor supply
- Terrain differentiator: Outdoor is Terrain's entire category, not an afterthought; deeper curation and verification standards for outdoor/adventure specifically; stronger guide community in North America

**REI Adventures**
- Positioning: Premium guided outdoor trips with REI brand trust
- ICP overlap: Adventurers seeking multi-day guided outdoor expeditions; higher-spend, more committed outdoor enthusiasts
- Take rate: N/A — direct model, not a marketplace
- Primary threat vector: Brand trust and co-op membership relationship with the outdoor enthusiast segment; the "safe" choice for adventurers who want guaranteed quality
- Current status: Stable; premium positioning, limited inventory, not a marketplace
- Terrain differentiator: Much broader supply at every price point; day experiences and short-format instruction (not just multi-day expeditions); individual guides, not just curated REI trips; more accessible price range

---

## Market Segments

**Terrain plays in:** Individual-guide, shorter-format outdoor experiences. Day experiences and half-day instruction in adventure/outdoor categories (surfing, climbing, hiking, kayaking, foraging, fishing). Local adventurers and destination travelers in North America.

**Terrain does not (yet) play in:** Multi-day expeditions and guided trips (REI Adventures, Wilderness Travel territory). Cultural and city-based experiences (food tours, historical walks — Viator/GetYourGuide territory). Corporate team events and group experiences at scale.

**Where competitors are strongest:**
- Demand aggregation: Airbnb Experiences has the strongest demand moat via accommodation cross-sell
- SEO and discovery at destination intent: Viator and GetYourGuide dominate search for "things to do in [city]"
- Premium multi-day: REI Adventures is trusted at the high end

---

## Terrain's Competitive Advantages

- **Outdoor-native curation:** Every guide is vetted for the outdoor/adventure category. Quality bar is higher than generalist platforms.
- **Independent guide supply:** Individual instructors can list and start booking in hours. No operator-scale requirement.
- **Category depth:** More outdoor experience types, more guides per outdoor category in North American markets than any single competitor.
- **Lower take rate than most competitors:** 18% versus 20–30% at Viator/GetYourGuide — meaningful for guides deciding where to list.

---

## Known Gaps

- **Demand moat:** No equivalent to Airbnb's accommodation traffic. Terrain must build standalone destination intent or become the go-to for local experience seekers — neither is fully established yet.
- **Brand recognition:** Viator and GetYourGuide show up first in organic search for "outdoor experiences near me." Terrain's SEO presence is improving but not competitive at scale.
- **Multi-day experiences:** Not in the product today. An emerging segment of high-intent adventurers (surf camps, mountaineering expeditions) is underserved.
- **Social proof at scale:** Review volume per listing is lower than Viator's catalog. New guides on Terrain face a cold-start credibility problem.
